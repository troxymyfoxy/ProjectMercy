Alert Description

There are a couple rules in Sanzu that will generate an Input Errors alert; steps to troubleshoot either are the same.

Possible Causes

There are a handful of potential causes for input errors. Issues with the physical infrastructure are most common: bad transceivers, dirty fiber, etc.

Escalation Plan

Any special escalation requirements will be added here as they develop, however, for the most part you will follow general network escalation steps. You should utilize this plan if you are uncertain of the impact of an event, or you are being overwhelmed with alerts.

Resolution

To get resolution you will be working either with the 3rd party NOC, our own Datacenter Operations team, or perhaps the vendor of the network device. These groups may ask you for information, some commands to provide commonly requested information is below. You can proactively provide it by adding it to the ticket you've opened, to expedite troubleshooting. It's difficult to list every scenario that will branch from troubleshooting this with these groups, so if you get a request you're uncertain of how to respond to, work with the rest of the GSOC to determine your best course of action. 

 Getting information commonly used for troubleshooting input errors

Validation

Once we believe the cause of the errors has been located and remedied, you can end the maintenance calendar event, putting production traffic back on the link, and monitor for errors. If we see errors increment again, you can revert back to your triage steps and continue troubleshooting with the 3rd party NOC. If the circuit is running clean, you can move your ticket to done, monitor for 24 hours, and close if stability continues.

Additional Information

You can also view all the interface errors per router by going to: Monocle Router CRC Errors. Remember to change the POP to the POP you are troubleshooting. If errors are being seen you should see them listed.
